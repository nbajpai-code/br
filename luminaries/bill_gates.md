# Bill Gates' Book Recommendations

Bill Gates is known for his voracious reading habits and his annual book lists on [Gates Notes](https://www.gatesnotes.com/). Here are some of his recent recommendations.

## 2025 Recommendations (Winter)
*   **Remarkably Bright Creatures** by Shelby Van Pelt
*   **Clearing the Air** by Hannah Ritchie
*   **Who Knew** by Barry Diller
*   **When Everyone Knows That Everyone Knows** by Steven Pinker
*   **Abundance** by Ezra Klein and Derek Thompson

## 2024 Recommendations
*   **The Women** by Kristin Hannah
*   **Infectious Generosity** by Chris Anderson
*   **Brave New Words** by Sal Khan
*   **How to Know a Person** by David Brooks
*   **An Unfinished Love Story: A Personal History of the 1960s** by Doris Kearns Goodwin
*   **The Anxious Generation: How the Great Rewiring of Childhood Is Causing an Epidemic of Mental Illness** by Jonathan Haidt
*   **Engineering in Plain Sight: An Illustrated Field Guide to the Constructed Environment** by Grady Hillhouse
*   **The Coming Wave: Technology, Power, and the Twenty-First Century's Greatest Dilemma** by Mustafa Suleyman
*   **Federer** by Doris Henkel (Bonus for tennis fans)

## 2023 Recommendations
*   **The Song of the Cell: An Exploration of Medicine and the New Human** by Siddhartha Mukherjee
*   **Not the End of the World: How We Can Be the First Generation to Build a Sustainable Planet** by Hannah Ritchie
*   **Invention and Innovation: A Brief History of Hype and Failure** by Vaclav Smil

## Other Notable Mentions
*   **Abundance** by Ezra Klein and Derek Thompson
*   **Remarkably Bright Creatures** by Shelby Van Pelt
*   **Clearing the Air** by Hannah Ritchie
